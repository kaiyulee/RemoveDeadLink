Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2025‑11‑17

What this extension does

Bookmark Dead Link Cleaner scans your bookmarks to find dead links and helps you remove them.

Data collection

  • No personal data is collected, transmitted, or sold.
  • All processing happens locally in your browser.

Data the extension handles

  • Bookmarks: The bookmarks tree is read to check URLs and, only on your explicit action, selected bookmarks can be removed.
  • Network checks: To verify reachability, the extension issues HEAD/GET requests to the bookmarked URLs you scan.
  • Domain status: Public RDAP and DNS‑over‑HTTPS (DoH) endpoints are queried to detect expired or non‑existent domains.
  • Local storage: Settings (timeout, concurrency, language, whitelist) and the latest scan results are stored locally with chrome.storage.local.

How data is used

  • Bookmarks and network responses are used only to determine whether a link is dead and to present results for your review.
  • Stored settings and last results exist solely to improve your experience and can be cleared anytime by removing the extension or clearing site data.

Sharing and third parties

No analytics, advertising, or tracking services are used. The extension does not share personal data with any party. Network requests are limited to:

  • Bookmarked sites you choose to scan (HEAD/GET).
  • Public RDAP endpoints (e.g., https://rdap.org/) to check domain status/expiration.
  • DNS‑over‑HTTPS endpoint https://dns.google/resolve to detect NXDOMAIN.

Returned content is not executed. No remote code is downloaded or run.

Permissions

  • bookmarks: Read bookmarks and remove selected items on your command.
  • storage: Save user settings and the last scan result locally.
  • <all_urls>: Issue requests to bookmarked URLs to verify reachability.

Data retention & deletion

  • All data remains on your device. No server‑side storage.
  • Remove the extension or clear browser storage to delete saved settings/results.
